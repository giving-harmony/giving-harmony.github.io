Giving Harmony

Playing notes of contribution

Giving Harmony is a non-profit organization founded by a group of young musicians with a heart for music and a voice raising awareness to their community. In establishing a sense of harmony, these musicians work to donate to different organizations each year, through concerts and other events. All proceeds are donated to help those in need.

Proceeds go to Guide Dogs of America

Katherine Wong, Guest Pianist

Katherine Wong is an undergraduate student at Stanford University studying Symbolic Systems (Computer Science + Psychology, Philosophy, and Linguistics) and English. In college, she is involved in Stanford's Chamber Music program, where she performs in a piano quartet. Katherine has been playing the piano since the age of five; at eight years old, Katherine made her debut at the Lincoln Center in New York City. Since then, she has performed at numerous prestigious concert halls around the world, including Carnegie Hall in New York City (4x), Mozarteum in Austria, Concertgebouw in the Netherlands, Megaron Concert Hall in Greece, and Parco della Musica in Italy. She has been recognized as a Panel Masters award winner in the MTAC Certificate of Merit Program, nominated as an Orange County Artist of the Year, named a California Young Artist Scholar, and awarded top winner prizes at the American Fine Arts Festival International Music Competition, Bremen International Piano Competition (Japan), North International Piano Competition (Sweden). In addition, Katherine was selected to perform a solo charity concert at the National Taipei National Concert Hall during summer of 2021 before COVID-19. She also plays the cello and has been recognized as a Level 10 Advanced Cello award winner of MTAC's Certificate of Merit Program.
